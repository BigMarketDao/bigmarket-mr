## 1. PLATFORM OVERVIEW

### What BigMarket is
A DAO-governed prediction market protocol on Stacks (Bitcoin-settled) where users trade on outcome probabilities using a hybrid CPMM + LP architecture with isolated per-market risk pools.

### Core value proposition
- **Zero-sum guarantee:** every token that enters exits only via winning claim, LP withdrawal, or protocol fees — no leakage
- **Market-isolated risk:** a bad market cannot contaminate others
- **Continuous pricing:** CPMM curve enables real-time buy/sell with no expiry on exiting a position
- **Permissionless participation:** anyone can trade; creators are DAO-gated
- **Hybrid execution:** off-chain coordination (order matching, AI tooling) + on-chain custody and settlement

### Blockchain/network
Stacks L2, Bitcoin-settled via Proof of Transfer (PoX). Cross-chain expansion to Solana and Sui is in the roadmap (Phase 3).

### Current status
Phase 2 in development. Testnet active. Mainnet deployment planned.

## 2. MARKET TYPES

### Market mechanism types (orthogonal to outcome type)

**MECHANISM_KNOCKOUT (`u1`) — Parimutuel mode**
- Buy and hold only — no selling, no LP
- At resolution, the winning pool is split proportionally among holders
- Simple zero-sum split; no continuous repricing
- Source: `bme024-0-market-predicting.clar:28–40`

**MECHANISM_AMM (`u2`) — CPMM mode (default)**
- Constant Product Market Maker pricing: `selected_pool × other_pool = k`
- Prices update continuously as users trade
- Buy, sell, and add/remove liquidity all supported
- Source: `bme024-0-market-predicting.clar:28–40`

### Binary markets
Single true/false question (e.g. "Will X happen?"). Two outcome pools. Resolved YES or NO. Either KNOCKOUT or AMM mechanism. Resolution via DAO resolution agent ± dispute.

### Scalar markets
Range-bounded numeric outcome (e.g. "BTC price on date X"). Uses Pyth price oracle for on-chain resolution. Contract: `bme024-0-market-scalar-pyth.clar`.

### Categorical markets
Multiple discrete outcomes (e.g. "Who will win?"). Implemented via `MARKET_TYPE u1` with N outcome pools. Full CPMM pricing across all categories.

### Resolution method (all types)
1. Resolution agent signals outcome via `bme008-0-resolution-coordinator`
2. **Dispute window opens** — 144 blocks (~1 day) by default
3. Any stakeholder can challenge → market enters `RESOLUTION_DISPUTED`
4. DAO vote (288 blocks, ~2.5 days) determines outcome
5. If no challenge → resolves automatically after window
6. Claims open once `RESOLUTION_RESOLVED` state is reached

## 3. TRADING MECHANICS

### How to place a bet
1. Connect Stacks wallet (Hiro/Xverse via WalletConnect)
2. Select a market and outcome category
3. Enter amount; UI shows estimated shares and effective price
4. Set slippage tolerance (min-shares parameter)
5. Submit `predict-category` transaction on-chain
6. Shares are minted to your address

### Tokens used for betting
SIP-010 fungible tokens; must be DAO-approved per market. Supported token list includes STX, sBTC, USDA, PEPE, wrapped STX. Each market is configured to accept one specific token.

### Fee structure

| Fee | Rate | Who receives | When |
|-----|------|-------------|------|
| Protocol fee (dev-fee-bips) | **1%** (100 bips) | Split below | On every buy or sell |
| → LP portion | **30% of protocol fee** (= 0.3% of trade) | Accumulated in pool; LPs claim via share value | On every trade |
| → Treasury/dev | **70% of protocol fee** (= 0.7% of trade) | DAO treasury | On every trade |
| Market creator fee | **Up to 10%** (creator-set, 0–1000 bips) | Market creator | On every trade in their market |

Source: `bme024-0-market-predicting.clar:100–102`

> **Note on fee stacking:** Creator fee and protocol fee are additive. A market with a 5% creator fee + 1% protocol fee means the user pays 6% total on the gross trade value.

### Payout calculation
Winner receives: `(user_winning_shares / total_winning_shares) × total_pool_value`

Processed only after market reaches `RESOLUTION_RESOLVED` state. No time limit on claiming.

### Selling / exiting early
**Yes — fully supported in AMM mode.** Users call `sell-category` at any time before the market closes. Refund is calculated via inverse CPMM formula based on current pool balances. Min-refund parameter provides slippage protection. KNOCKOUT mode markets **do not** support selling.

### Liquidity provision

**Adding liquidity**
- Call `add-liquidity` with an amount and slippage guard (`max-deviation-bips`)
- Liquidity is added **proportionally across ALL outcome pools simultaneously** — no directional bet
- Available any time while market is OPEN
- Any user with the market's token can participate (no permissions required)
- Anti-sandwich: transaction reverts if pool has shifted beyond `expected-total-stakes ± max-deviation-bips`

**Risks**
- Impermanent loss if outcome prices diverge significantly
- Cannot withdraw single-category liquidity — must maintain balanced position

**Rewards**
- Earn the 0.3% LP portion of every trade proportional to LP share
- Reputation (BIG-R) points for LP activity

**Removing liquidity**
- Call `remove-liquidity`; withdraws proportionally from all pools
- Protected via `min-refund` slippage parameter

Source: `docs/specifications/liquidity-provision.md`

## 4. MARKET CREATION

### Who can create markets
DAO-gated via `bme022-0-market-gating` extension. Gating can use allowlists or Merkle proofs. Not permissionless — requires DAO approval to be a creator.

### Creation requirements
- **Creation fee:** DAO-configurable; exact STX amount not hardcoded in current contracts — **TBD**
- **BIG stake requirement:** Not required in current contract implementation (BIG is governance/reputation only)
- **Approval process:** Must be on DAO-approved creator list

### Creator earnings
- Set custom market fee at creation: **0–10%** of every trade in the market
- Paid automatically on each trade transaction
- Reputation (BIG-R) points earned for creating active markets

### Market parameters (what creator sets)
Title, description, outcome categories, trading token, market duration (default 144 blocks), creator fee rate (0–1000 bips), mechanism type (AMM or KNOCKOUT), resolution agent address.

### AI market generator
Referenced in planning docs as an off-chain API feature. No Clarity contract implementation found. Likely generates market parameters via LLM and submits them via the SDK.

## 6. REPUTATION SYSTEM (BIG-R / BIGR)

### What BIG-R is
A soul-bound SIP-013 semi-fungible token (BIGR) representing earned participation in the BigMarket ecosystem. Non-transferable. Converts to BIG each epoch. Multiple token IDs can be held by a single user.

### How it's earned

**Known earning actions (from contract minting calls):**
| Action | Source |
|--------|--------|
| Providing liquidity | LP extension |
| Trading (buying/selling shares) | Market predicting extension |
| Participating in dispute resolution votes | Market voting extension |
| Signaling market resolution | Resolution coordinator |

> **Gap:** Exact point values per action are not centralized in one constants file — each extension mints independently. No complete reward table found.

### How it converts to BIG
- Each **epoch** (4,000 blocks ~monthly on mainnet), a pool of **10,000 BIG** is distributed
- User calls `claim-tier-rep-reward` at epoch end
- BIG received = `(user_weighted_reputation / total_weighted_reputation_that_epoch) × 10,000`
- Users can backfill claims for up to **12 past epochs**

### Tier weights
- **20 tiers** (0–20), configurable by DAO
- Higher tier = higher weight multiplier per BIGR unit when calculating epoch claim
- Exact tier weight values: **DAO-controlled, not hardcoded** — set via governance

### Soul-bound mechanics
Transfer is blocked at contract level with `err-soulbound`. Can only be minted by DAO-approved extensions. Cannot be sold, traded, or transferred. Can be burned via explicit burn functions only.

### Early contributor bonus
Not documented in contracts or docs.

## 7. DISPUTE SYSTEM

### How disputes work (full process)

1. Resolution agent signals outcome → market enters `RESOLUTION_RESOLVING`
2. **144-block dispute window** opens (~1 day)
3. Any market participant (must hold stake) calls `dispute-resolution`
4. Market transitions to `RESOLUTION_DISPUTED`
5. DAO market voting contract opens a **288-block vote** (~2.5 days)
6. Governance votes on correct outcome
7. Winning outcome is applied → `RESOLUTION_RESOLVED`
8. If no dispute in window → auto-resolves after timeout

### Who can dispute
Must hold a non-zero stake in the market (`err-disputer-must-have-stake` if not). No BIG requirement. No deposit required.

### Dispute window
`u144` blocks default (~1 Bitcoin day). DAO-configurable via `set-dispute-window-length`. Source: `bme024-0-market-predicting.clar:99`

### Voting on disputes
DAO governance vote. Duration: `u288` blocks (~2.5 days). Token-weighted voting. Quorum and majority threshold: proposal-defined.

### Penalties / incentives
- **Disputing:** No financial penalty — access-gated (must have stake). No slashing found.
- **Voting:** Voters earn BIGR reputation for participating in resolution votes.
- **Incorrect resolution agent:** DAO can vote to override; no explicit penalty contract found.

## 8. TREASURY

### What the treasury is
The DAO-controlled contract (`bme006-0-treasury.clar`) that holds all protocol assets: STX, SIP-010 fungible tokens, SIP-009 NFTs, and SIP-013 semi-fungibles. All spending requires DAO proposal execution.

### Revenue sources
1. **Protocol fee** — 70% of the 1% dev fee on all trades flows to treasury
2. **Market creation fees** — if set by DAO (amount TBD)
3. **Donations / direct transfers** — open to any sender

### Fee distribution (verified)

From each trade:
- **1% protocol fee** collected
  - **30% → LP pool** (stays in market, increases LP share value)
  - **70% → DAO treasury**

> The "40% dev / 30% DAO / 20% staking / 10% buyback" split is **not** found in current contracts. The only verified split is 30% LP / 70% treasury from the 1% protocol fee.

### Treasury algorithm / hedge mechanism
Optional hedge strategy contract (`bme032-0-scalar-strategy-hedge.clar`) can be activated per market during the cool-down period. Allows treasury to manage directional exposure. Can be toggled off by DAO. Exact algorithm: not fully documented publicly.

### Treasury transparency
All treasury operations are on-chain Clarity contracts. Governance proposals required for spending. Observable via Stacks explorer.

## 13. DAO PAGE BUILD RECOMMENDATIONS

### Top 5 features to highlight on the DAO landing page
*(ranked by clarity to a non-crypto user + differentiation from competitors)*

1. **Market-isolated risk** — "A bad market can't affect your other positions." Simple, powerful, unique. Most competitors have shared liquidity pools.
2. **Soul-bound reputation (BIGR → BIG)** — "Earn governance tokens by participating — you can't buy influence, only earn it." Highly differentiating vs. pure token-weighted governance.
3. **Sell your position anytime** — "Changed your mind? Exit before the market closes." AMM mode continuous exit removes the "stuck holding a losing bet" UX problem.
4. **10,000 BIG per month to active participants** — Concrete, quantified reward. Easy to understand. "The protocol pays you to use it."
5. **DAO controls everything on-chain** — "No company controls the fee rate, who can create markets, or where revenue goes." Trust differentiation vs. centralized prediction markets.

### Top 3 features to AVOID leading with on the DAO landing page

1. **Treasury hedge mechanism** — Too technical, unexplained, and trust-reducing ("the DAO is trading with fees?"). Deprioritize.
2. **CPMM / AMM mechanism** — Jargon. Leads with how it works rather than why it matters. Replace with outcome ("accurate prices, exit anytime").
3. **Clarity smart contracts / Stacks architecture** — Niche audience. Most newcomers don't care. Move to a technical deep-dive section.

### Proposed DAO control panel tabs
*(based on features that actually exist)*

| Tab | Content |
|-----|---------|
| **Overview** | Treasury balance, active markets, epoch countdown, current fee rates |
| **Proposals** | Active votes, past proposals, submit new proposal |
| **Reputation** | Your BIGR balance, tier, epoch claim, leaderboard |
| **Markets** | Creator management, gating allowlist, market stats |
| **Treasury** | Asset breakdown, revenue history, spending proposals |
| **Parameters** | Current on-chain constants (fee rates, windows, epoch length) — read-only view |

### Missing features that would make the DAO page more compelling
1. **Real-time epoch progress bar** — show how much of the 10,000 BIG pool is allocated and when the next claim window opens. High engagement potential.
2. **Transparent fee flow visualization** — show live: trade volume → 1% fee → 30% LP / 70% treasury. Makes the token economy tangible.
3. **Creator leaderboard** — markets created, volume generated, reputation earned. Drives creator competition and social proof.
4. **BIG staking / yield contract** — currently no staking mechanism exists; adding it would make BIG more compelling to hold.
5. **Token allocation public breakdown** — the 98.5% unallocated supply is a trust risk. Publishing the full allocation plan (even as a proposal) would address the most common investor question.
